ABOUT P2P

Here you will find answers to some of the most popular P2P-related questions from prospective investors.



What is Peer-to-peer lending?

Peer-to-peer lending is the lending of money to third-parties through online services that match lenders with borrowers. As peer-to-peer lending companies offering these services tend to have be online, they can run with reduced costs and provide the service for less than traditional financial institutions. Because of this, lenders can earn more than they would with traditional services offered by banks, while borrowers can borrow money at lower interest rates.

Is it safe? Could I lose all my money?

EXPERIENCE P2P currently invests in products which have never lost money. In fact, none of them have earned any less than the projected rate. However, P2P is not risk free. Unlike a traditional bank, there is no government protection on unforseen losses should the worst happen. This is why we only recommend products that we have invested in ourselves. It is strongly advised that investors only use money they are not reliant on for their needs. If you are currently saving for a house or your child's university fund, it is best to use a strategy with more protection.

How does tax work?

Income earned through P2P lending is taxable and should be declared. P2P companies will send you an annual statement which shows how much you have earned. The good news for many people is that the Personal Savings Allowance is £1000 for lower bracket earners and £500 for higher earners, meaning unless you are investing tens of thousands you will not pay any tax.
